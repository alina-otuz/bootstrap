Olympic History

The ancient Olympic Games began in 776 BC in Olympia, Greece. The modern Olympics were revived in 1896 in Athens by Pierre de Coubertin, creating a legacy of international sporting excellence.

Frequently Asked Questions

The next Winter Olympics will be held in Milano-Cortina, Italy in 2026 (February 6-22). The next Summer Olympics will take place in Los Angeles, USA in 2028.

Baron Pierre de Coubertin, a French educator and historian, founded the modern Olympic Games. The first modern Olympics were held in Athens, Greece in 1896, reviving the ancient Greek tradition.

The Olympic Games are held every four years, alternating between Summer and Winter Games. This means there's an Olympic Games (either Summer or Winter) every two years.

"Citius, Altius, Fortius – Communiter" is Latin for "Faster, Higher, Stronger – Together." The word "Together" was added in 2021 to emphasize solidarity and unity in the Olympic spirit.
